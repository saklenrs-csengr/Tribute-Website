A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 at Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam played pivotal roles in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was instrumental in the development of the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which made India a nuclear power.

After completing his studies in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, Dr. Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he made remarkable contributions to India's space and missile programs. He also served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, during which he was affectionately known as the "People's President" due to his humble nature and dedication to the youth of India.

In addition to his work in science and technology, Dr. Kalam was a visionary thinker and a passionate advocate for education. He emphasized the importance of nurturing young minds and inspiring future generations to dream big and work hard. His book Wings of Fire became a bestseller and continues to motivate millions around the world.
